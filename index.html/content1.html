1.Angiosperms (Flowering Plants):

  1. Monocots: Plants with one cotyledon in their seed (e.g., grasses, lilies, orchids).
  2. Dicots:Plants with two cotyledons in their seed (e.g., roses, sunflowers, maple trees).


2.Gymnosperms (Non-Flowering Seed Plants):

  1. Conifers:Cone-bearing plants (e.g., pine, spruce, fir).
  2. Cycads: Palm-like plants (e.g., sago palm).



3.Ferns and Allies:

  1. Ferns:Plants that reproduce via spores instead of seeds
  2. Mosses and Liverworts: Simple, non-vascular plants.


4.Bryophytes:

  1. Mosses: Small, non-vascular plants.
  2. Liverworts: simple plants with leafy structures





5. Herbaceous Plants:

  1. Annuals: Complete their life cycle in one growing season (e.g., marigolds, petunias)
